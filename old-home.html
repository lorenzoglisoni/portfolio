THE PROBLEM

As a consequence of a fast expansion, relations between members of Nest Collective, a co-working space in Portugal, have started decreasing: it has became frequent for co-workers to not know each other. The space is at risk of loosing its community-oriented character, a trait that stays at the core of the business.

We sat down with the founders to create a solution able to improve the quality of relations between co-workers.

MY ROLE

I worked as Product Designer in a team of six interns. I led user research phases: setting, running and documenting interviews and observations. Equally dividing responsibilities with the team, I ideated, prototyped (in Figma) and conducted user tests of the platform. I supported in the implementation of the platform front end (HTML, SASS).

INSIGHTS · design sprint

What did we learn

· Communication about events is not well planned

Different  channels are used to share events (Slack, FB, Linkedin, mail, physical board in the office).  As a result, the attendance rate of events is low. Users also reported a mismatch between their interests and the events offered. 

· Hard to obtain relevant infos about other members

Hard to get to know everyone. Slack does not provide any personal information. Facebook is not used for work-related relationships, and Linkedin is mainly used for business purposes. 

· Managing spaces is problematic

There is not a single person responsible for the management of spaces. This creates a messy organisation of rooms occupancy, that gets critical during busy times.


· No central source of trust

World of mouth is the main source about written and un-written rules of the space. However, new joiners are struggling in understanding the mechanisms and are often afraid to ask.

IDEATION · design sprint

Defining opportunity areas

We decided to focus on solving problems that emerged more frequently as a result of research. They were the ones with the highest value for users and stakeholders.

We choose to tackle the issues related to managing events, the need of a richer profile and the lack of a central source of trust.

To help us in directing our creative effort into the right opportunity areas, we reframed our insight statements as How Might We questions:

How can we improve events sharing, and increase attendance rate?
How might we centralize general info about the companies? And make managing spaces easy?
How might we ensure that all the users fill reach profiles?

Choose what to build

The all team, designers and developers, sat down together and brainstormed over possible solutions. We used techniques such as Crazy 8’s and body-storming to come up with ideas.

DESIGN SPRINT

End of the sprint

The Design Sprint terminated after testing session: in five days we discovered problems, created solutions and tested them with users.

Our assumption about the onboarding process was validated.

What we had in our hands was a low fidelity prototype of the registration process, a list of improvements to be applied to it coming from testing sessions, and a backlog of new future features to be built over the upcoming weeks.

Let’s start building!

It was then time to start building the platform. We started from the registration process.

Over the following nine weeks, following an agile way of working, we designed, tested and implemented different additional features of the platform.

We created profile pages for companies and co-workers , a search functionality, an address book, personal profile page, Slack login implementation, a landing page.

How was the design and development process structured?

When time to tackle a new feature, the design team prototyped it in Figma. The list of features to prioritise during the process changed, following the unpredictable nature of a project. The design was based on the style guide that was defined at the beginning of the development process. This helped the design team to move fast.

The first designs ideas were tested immediately with users,  co-workers or team members. This allowed us to maintain a continuous testing process and reduce errors and time wasted.

The initial prototype was then refined based on the results of testing. Usability issues were solved at this stage. A final visual polishment applied. The prototype was ready to be implemented by the dev team.

Results

The final outcome resulted in a working platform that we presented to the Collective. Some features were lacking, due to time and resources constraints, but the vast majority of the platform was up and running.

The vast majority of users strongly appreciated it. Indeed, one month after the end of the project more than 60% of co-workers have created a profile in the platform. Events that were shared via the platform saw an increases in the attendance rate.
